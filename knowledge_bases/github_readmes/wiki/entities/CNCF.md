## Overview
The Cloud Native Computing Foundation (CNCF) is a nonprofit organization under the Linux Foundation that hosts and governs open-source projects enabling cloud-native software development. CNCF was founded in 2015 to standardize and accelerate container-packaged, dynamically scheduled, and microservices-oriented software. Kubernetes is its founding project and flagship offering.

## Key Facts
- **Founded**: 2015 by Google and the Linux Foundation
- **Host projects**: Kubernetes (founding), Prometheus, Envoy, gRPC, Helm, Thanos, Argo CD, and hundreds more
- **Governance model**: Projects progress through sandbox → incubating → graduated tiers based on maturity criteria
- **Mission**: To make cloud-native computing universal and sustainable through open-source, vendor-neutral projects
- **Kubernetes role**: Kubernetes is the CNCF's flagship project, demonstrating the foundation's commitment to production-grade, community-driven infrastructure software
